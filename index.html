My story


Beginning

I was born on 16th September 1970 in Zugdidi, Georgia. A small and skinny child, I grew up in the streets of Old Station, a neighbourhood of my town with lots of urban stadiums. In my first tenth, I noticed competitive spirit, which all of my older friends had. They had a dream, once to play in front of all Georgians and make them proud.


First years as pro

At the age of 16, I joined Odishi Football Club. From there began my path as a pro footballer. First season was tough: I couldn't get enough playtime to prove myself, but following year was a bit luckier. With an injury of the main Centre-Back - Luka Norakidze - I finally got opportunities to show my skills in real games. Everything went smooth: tackle after tackle, clearance after clearance I was becoming better.



First and last move

As everything, good times also have an end. It was time for me to perform on a higher level. My two-season journey in Odishi was over and I moved to Dinamo Tbilisi FC. Even though I struggled a lot at the beginning, in half-season I got primary spot as Centre-Back. As my long-time teammate - Giorgi Gvinjilia loved to say: "Attack wins you matches, defence wins you titles."
